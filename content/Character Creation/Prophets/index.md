**Prophets** are heroes that call upon divine forces to support their allies. If you want to help your friends do their best and wield great power at the risk of not receiving it when you need it most, play a **Prophet**.

### Classes
There are 3 Classes of **Prophet**:
- **[[Druid]]**, representatives of nature itself who turn the environment against the enemy.
- **[[Priest]]**, children of divine beings who call upon their power to bolster and heal their allies.
- **[[Seer]]**, oracles who use the art of divination to prepare their party for what awaits them.
### Fate Die
During their adventures, a **Prophet**’s connection to their divine force is represented by their **Fate** **Die**. A Tier I **Prophet**’s **Fate Die** is a d8, becoming a d10 at Tier II, and a d12 at Tier III.  

A **Prophet** uses their **Fate Die** to call upon **Miracles** (detailed below).  

Additionally, when a **Prophet** would take damage, they roll their **Fate Die**. On an 2 or higher, divine intervention leaves the **Prophet** unharmed. Each time a **Prophet** avoids damage this way, the number required to avoid the damage goes up by 1 (to a maximum of the number of faces on their **Fate Die**).  
### Miracles
When you want to call upon your divine force, you call upon a **Miracle**.  

To call upon a **Miracle**, roll your **Fate Die**. Compare the result to the **Miracle**’s **Fate Target** to determine the effect of the **Miracle**. If the **Fate Die** rolled lower than the **Fate Target**, the Standard Effect takes place. If the **Fate Die** rolls a number greater than or equal to the **Fate Target** however, the more powerful **Divine Effect** takes place.  

You can only call upon **[[Miracles]]** granted by your Class or Talents.  

**Miracles** are separated into 3 Tiers, corresponding to the Tier of your character. You can only call upon a **Miracle** if you are of the corresponding Tier or higher.
