Welcome to ***BOUNDLESS*** the Tabletop Roleplaying Game. 
#### Goals of the Game
***BOUNDLESS*** sets out to be a game that allows the players to explore heroic fantasy stories with powerful, unrealistic characters. Warriors who can leap over mountains, spellcasters who reshape reality, etc.

Despite characters being unrealistically powerful, we aim to maintain tension and threat of defeat. In ***BOUNDLESS***, a knife is always fatal if it can find its mark. As players get more powerful and deal out more damage, as do their enemies. The players' health (represented by HP) however, does not scale up for most characters. This may sound strange at first, however all characters have some way of avoiding being harmed. And so although a knife is always fatal, it becomes increasingly unlikely for it to deal its damage.
#### Content Warning & Safety Tools
Playing tabletop roleplaying games is meant to be a fun and comfortable experience for everyone involved, and so here we provide you with some resources to ensure everyone at your table (whether it’s physical or digital) feels safe and welcome.

In ***BOUNDLESS***, we explore a story of the players going on grand adventures together. In many games, this may have the risk of the player characters getting seriously hurt, or even meet their ultimate end. This is a possibility we assume to be present when designing this game, as it can bring great tension and provide risk. At some tables however, this might be an unwelcome aspect of the game, and so you may wish to work with your GM to use the content and mechanics in this book that work for you, and leave the rest off your table.

There is however the possibility that we fail to foresee any potential problems that may arise at the table, and so the amazing tabletop community has developed what we call “safety tools” to address this. Popular examples of safety tools include the X-card ([link here](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1SB0jsx34bWHZWbnNIVVuMjhDkrdFGo1_hSC2BWPlI3A/edit)) and lines and veils. Lines and veils is a technique that involves the players and GM noting down themes or topics they don’t want to come up at the table (lines) and some themes or topics that are sometimes okay depending on execution (veils).
